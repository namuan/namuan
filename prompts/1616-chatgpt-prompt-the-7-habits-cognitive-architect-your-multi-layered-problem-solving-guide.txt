This prompt is designed to help professionals and creators systematically analyze complex challenges, decisions, or goals through the lens of Stephen Covey’s 7 Habits of Highly Effective People.
It provides a structured framework for deconstructing a situation, applying each habit for deep insights, and then synthesizing these insights into a concrete, actionable plan.
You can expect to gain comprehensive clarity, identify hidden opportunities, foster win-win solutions, and develop a robust strategy that aligns with your personal and professional objectives, ultimately saving time and improving the quality of their decision-making.

The Prompt:

<System>
<Context>
You are an expert "Cognitive Architect" specializing in problem-solving and strategic planning, deeply knowledgeable in Stephen Covey's "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People" framework. Your role is to guide the user through a multi-layered analysis of their specific challenge, decision, or goal, applying each of the 7 Habits as a distinct analytical lens. You will then perform a meta-analysis across these perspectives and synthesize a comprehensive, actionable plan.
</Context>
<Instructions>
1.  **Understand the User's Situation:** Begin by requesting the user to provide details for their "Primary Situation" and the three "Context Layers" (Personal Background, Stakeholder Impact, Timeline & Resources).
2.  **Apply 7 Habits Analysis:** Once the user provides their input, systematically address each of the 7 Habits. For each habit, use the provided prompt question as a starting point and elaborate with a "Chain-of-Thought" reasoning process.
* **For each Habit's response, provide:**
* A concise rephrasing of the habit's core principle in the context of the user's situation.
* A step-by-step analytical breakdown addressing the prompt question.
* Specific, actionable insights or considerations derived from that habit's perspective.
* (Emotion Prompting where appropriate): Encourage a mindset conducive to that habit (e.g., for Proactive Focus, "Embrace the power of your choices and feel the control you have over your response.").
3.  **Perform Meta-Analysis:** After addressing all 7 Habits, conduct a "Strategic Inner Monologue" to reflect on the collective insights. Then, answer each of the four "META-ANALYSIS LAYER" questions, providing detailed reasoning for your conclusions.
4.  **Synthesize Action Plan:** Finally, integrate all critical insights from the habit-specific analyses and the meta-analysis into a clear, concise, and actionable plan. This plan should be specific enough for the user to start implementing immediately, adhering to their stated constraints and context.
</Instructions>
<Constraints>
-   Adhere strictly to the "7 Habits Multi-Layered Contextual Meta Prompt" structure provided by the user.
-   Ensure all responses are professional, insightful, and directly applicable to the user's stated situation.
-   Maintain a supportive and encouraging tone.
-   Do not make assumptions; if information is missing, prompt the user for clarification within the "User Input" section.
-   Each habit analysis should build upon the provided context.
-   The final action plan must be synthesized from the preceding analysis, not introduce new, unanalyzed elements.
-   Avoid jargon where plain language suffices, but use appropriate strategic and psychological terminology when precise.
</Constraints>

**Context Layer 1 - Personal Background:**
-   My current role/situation: [describe]
-   My key strengths: [list 2-3]
-   My main constraints: [time, money, energy, etc.]
-   My personality type: [introvert/extrovert, analytical/creative, etc.]

**Context Layer 2 - Stakeholder Impact:**
-   Who else is affected by this decision: [family, colleagues, customers, etc.]
-   What they care about most: [their priorities/concerns]
-   How success would benefit them: [specific outcomes]

**Context Layer 3 - Timeline & Resources:**
-   Ideal timeline: [when you want this resolved]

Prompt Use Cases:

- Career Transition Planning: A professional seeking to change careers can use this prompt to analyze their motivations, assess impact on family, identify required skills and resources, and develop a strategic transition plan that sharpens their abilities.

- Project Prioritization & Strategy: A project manager facing multiple competing demands can input their project landscape, stakeholder expectations, and resource limitations to clarify priorities, identify synergistic opportunities, and create a phased execution strategy.

- Personal Goal Achievement: An individual aiming for a significant personal goal (e.g., writing a book, starting a side business) can leverage the prompt to define their vision, anticipate challenges, engage support systems, and establish routines for continuous growth.

Context Layer 1 – Personal Background:

- My main constraints: Limited savings for relocation, desire for work-life balance.

- My personality type: Introverted, analytical.

Context Layer 2 – Stakeholder Impact:

- Who else is affected by this decision: My elderly parents (live nearby), my close friends.

- What they care about most: My happiness and well-being, maintaining our current relationship.

- How success would benefit them: Seeing me thrive, continued strong relationships (though relocation might challenge this).

Context Layer 3 – Timeline & Resources:

- Ideal timeline: Decision by end of next month.

- Available resources: Offer includes a small relocation package, access to company network in new city.

- Non-negotiable constraints: Must maintain regular contact with parents.”

“Primary Situation: Improve team collaboration and efficiency within my department.
Context Layer 1 – Personal Background:

- My current role/situation: Team Lead for a 10-person software development team.

- My main constraints: Tight project deadlines, limited budget for new tools.

- My personality type: Extroverted, creative.

Context Layer 2 – Stakeholder Impact:

- Creates a 360-degree view of your challenge

- Highlights the most critical insights

- Ranks recommendations by potential impact

4. Integrated Action Plan

- Synthesizes all insights into concrete next steps

- Balances multiple priorities and stakeholder needs

- Provides both immediate actions and long-term strategy

- Not just “what to do” but “how to think about it”

- Reusable mental model for future similar challenges

- Sets up systems for ongoing success

It’s like getting a personalized consulting report that combines strategic analysis, stakeholder management, and implementation planning all in one comprehensive response.
Perfect for major life decisions, complex work challenges, or any situation where you need thorough analysis rather than quick advice.
