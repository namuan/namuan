Step 3: Structure Your Prompt Framework üèóÔ∏è

Learning Concept
Well-structured prompts follow a clear formula: Role + Context + Task + Format + Example (when helpful).

Your Task
Build your prompt using this proven structure:
Prompt Template:

Act as a [ROLE] for [BRAND/COMPANY TYPE].

Context: [AUDIENCE + SITUATION + BRAND PERSONALITY]

Task: Create [SPECIFIC MARKETING ASSET] that [DESIRED OUTCOME].

Requirements:
- [FORMAT CONSTRAINT 1]
- [FORMAT CONSTRAINT 2] 
- [TONE/STYLE REQUIREMENT]
- [SPECIFIC ELEMENTS TO INCLUDE]

Focus on: [KEY MESSAGE/BENEFIT TO HIGHLIGHT]

Real Example

Act as a social media manager for a boutique fitness studio.

Context: Creating content for busy professionals who want effective workouts but struggle with time constraints. Brand is energetic, supportive, and results-focused.

Task: Create Instagram captions that motivate followers to try quick workout routines.

Requirements:
- 150-200 words maximum
- Include a clear call-to-action
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags
- Maintain an encouraging, energetic tone
- Reference specific time constraints (15-30 minutes)

Focus on: How small time investments can yield big fitness results.

Learning Concept
